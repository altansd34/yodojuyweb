The scientific meaning of two rainbows

The double rainbow's significance lies in its attractive beauty and the complex interplay of sunlight and raindrops. It happens because sunlight reflects twice inside a raindrop. First, sunlight bends when it enters a raindrop, separating colours.

The colours then reflect off the back of the raindrop toward the sun, creating a rainbow. The first rainbow shows colours backwards, and the second reflection fixes the pattern. It is similar to how words on a shirt look backwards in a mirror and get corrected when you see that reflection in a second mirror.

Mythological double rainbow symbolism

Myths vary across cultures. In some cultures, a double rainbow is seen as a bridge between the mortal and divine realms, symbolising a connection between the physical and spiritual realms.

It may also represent a message from beyond the physical or a sign of divine approval. The dual arches are sometimes associated with twin deities or contrasting forces in mythology, reflecting the duality inherent in the natural and supernatural realms.

For instance, in Norse mythology, a double rainbow symbolises Bifrost, while the Irish believe that seeing a double rainbow means leprechauns are near. On the other hand, Celtic associates the appearance of this natural phenomenon with paths to different realms. This means that the rainbows are bridges to greater divine power.
